Impact Evaluation
2019 - 2024

Cash-Based Transfers (CBT) and Gender Impact Evaluation - Isiolo County, Kenya

Project Coordinator

As Field Coordinator for the World Bank’s Development Impact Evaluation (DIME) initiative and the World Food Programme (WFP), I led field operations for a large-scale impact evaluation assessing the outcomes of cash-based transfer interventions and their gender-related impacts. My work involved translating research objectives into practical implementation strategies, supervising data collection, coordinating with county government and community stakeholders, and monitoring cash transfer activities to ensure adherence to evaluation protocols. I played a central role in community mobilization, training facilitation, and real-time field reporting—helping deliver high-quality data and actionable insights that informed program design and gender-responsive policy recommendations.

Project Management
2019 - 2022

Smart Matatu – Public Transport Safety Monitoring and Rating (Nairobi, Kenya)

Consultant Project Manager

As Deployment Team Lead at Echo Mobile, I managed a pioneering road safety project in collaboration with the University of California, Berkeley Economics Research team. The Smart Matatu initiative developed and deployed in-vehicle sensor technology to monitor driving behavior in Kenya’s minibus (matatu) industry. By analyzing sensor data and passenger feedback, our team generated evidence-based safety ratings that empowered commuters to identify safer transport options and promoted accountability among vehicle owners and SACCOs. I oversaw project deployment, stakeholder coordination, enumerator supervision, and data analysis—delivering a model now recognized for its innovation in improving public transport safety through data-driven insights.

Research
2019 - 2022

Research Project: “E-Commerce Behavioral and SME Onboarding Studies – UC Berkeley & Jumia Kenya

Research Project Manager

As Research Project Manager, I led two high-impact studies in collaboration with Jumia Kenya and the University of California, Berkeley Economics Research Team. The first explored how customer service agent gender influenced user engagement and purchasing behavior on the Jumia platform, while the second assessed how shifting SME onboarding from remote to on-site training affected seller performance and platform adoption. I coordinated end-to-end project execution—research design, data collection, and analysis—and translated findings into actionable recommendations that enhanced customer satisfaction, SME integration, and operational efficiency within Jumia’s e-commerce ecosystem.
